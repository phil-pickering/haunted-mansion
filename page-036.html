You run down the stairs and enter the library, where you almost collide with a slim, young woman in a black dress with white lace and a little white hat.

“You gave me such a start,” she says.

“I’m sorry about that,” you reply, “but I’m trying to find my cousin Jane. Who are you?”

“My name is Lena. I am Mrs. Bigley’s maid. Do you know that there is a policeman outside? I just saw him through the window. There is a boy with him. He’ll be coming in the back door. I must let him in.”

“Yes, he’ll help me find Jane,” you say.

“He will never be able to help you. You must stay out of sight. That is your only chance.”

Lena seems to make no sense; yet somehow you feel she is speaking the truth.
