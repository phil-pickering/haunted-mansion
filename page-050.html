You walk along a passageway that leads to a large hall. The floor is covered with oriental rugs, and the walls are lined with antique oil paintings. A grandfather clock slowly ticks the minutes away, its long brass pendulum swinging back and forth.

Suddenly, a voice from upstairs shatters the silence. “Melissa, Melissa!”

You start up the stairs, determined to find out who it is. When you reach the top, everything is silent. Then you hear a cough from one of the bedrooms. The door is ajar. You look in and see a very old woman, sitting in a rocking chair, knitting.

“Come in, come in,” she calls in a cheery voice.

“Are you Mrs. Bigley?” you ask.

The woman smiles and laughs. “No, No,” she says. “I’m her sister, Mrs. Krim; Mrs. Bigley is my cat.”
