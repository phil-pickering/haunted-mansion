You grope your way along in the dark, trying to find the swinging door to the kitchen. A hand clutches your arm. You feel like screaming.

“Help,” a voice calls almost in a whisper.

“Who are you?” you reply.

“I’m Lena, Mrs. Bigley’s maid. I was afraid to speak before. I cannot escape without your help.”

“You can just walk out of the house,” you say.

“I wish I could, but I can’t. I’m under the curse . . .”

“But can’t Mrs. Bigley help?”

“Mrs. Bigley? Don’t you understand?”

Lena bursts into tears. You try to comfort her. Finally, she steadies herself and renews her grip on your sleeve.

“Please,” she cries. “If you will only carry the cat out of the house, I will be saved. Will you help me?”
