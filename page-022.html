You cross the meadow to the cottage and knock loudly on the door.

In a moment a heavyset man wearing a dirty plaid shirt opens the door. His face is pale and fat. His eyes are pink, like a pig’s. Behind him is a huge dog—a mastiff—chained to a radiator. The dog growls menacingly, but does not move.

“What do you want?” Jervis asks gruffly.

“My cousin Jane is trapped in the house and I’m worried about her. Would you please unlock it and help me find her?”

“She should not be in there. Even I don’t set foot in that house,” Jervis says.

“Why not?” you ask.

“The mistress would put me away.”

“You mean you just take care of the grounds?”

“Aye, and bring them food and things.”

“But who takes care of the house?”

“It’s taken care of all right, and so is anybody who goes inside. That’s why I’ll let you in, if you want.”
