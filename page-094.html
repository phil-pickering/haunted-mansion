You walk up to the cupboard and fling open the door. On the shelf in front of your eyes is a stuffed mouse, surrounded by twigs and leaves as if in its natural setting. The mouse is standing with one tiny paw resting on a piece of bark. Your mouth falls open in disbelief when you read the brass plaque next to the mouse on which is inscribed Horace I. Bigley (1921–1979).

Bewildered and confused, you stagger out of Chimney Rock. A few minutes later, two more policemen arrive. They search the house, but they are unable to find Jane. You have a sinking feeling you may never see her again.
